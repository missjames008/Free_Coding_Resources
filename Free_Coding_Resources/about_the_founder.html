About the Founder

Jocelyn James is a web developer and designer with a background in human rights and a passion for utilising technology for social good. She obtained an MA in Understanding and Securing Human Rights from the University of London in 2010, and has gone on to work in multiple fields including mental health, higher education, and government.
 
Jocelyn believes that technology is for everyone, and that the growing opportunities in this sector should be made available to as many people as possible. This belief led to the creation of ETA: {NOW}, a platform to connect traditionally underrepresented populations (specifically, refugees and women) with free and affordable resources to help them enter the tech sector.
